Project Overview

About the LOVE night market festival

A 10-week project that dived into the visual systems for a music festival held at the University of Washington. This festival was a farmers market with a theme of romanticism; these values were incorporated throughout the design.

Roles UI/UX Designer, Visual Designer
Timeline 10 Weeks
Team Bethany (me)
Tools Figma
(02)

Wordmark

Visual identity through typography

As I worked on my final word mark I considered the feeling my wordmark would convey. In order to make this easier I had some requirements the font had to meet:

  • A feeling of comfort and warmth.
  • A close relationship with the word “Love.”

Finding a font that reflects my requirements was difficult. I had to spend hours searching for the perfect one. I eventually stumbled upon a font called “Tapestry”. This font features sharp edges that reminded me of the sharpness that can be found on rose petals. This immediately made me associate the font with the word “Love”. Additionally to emphasize the feeling of warmth and comfort I decided to use tracking to have individual characters closer.

Design that did not work:

Throughout my iteration I also worked on finding a way to associate the word mark via an analogy. I did this by making the ‘O’ in the word ‘LOVE’ a rose. Since roses are deeply associated with love I felt it was appropriate to incorporate it. However, after going through several rounds of critique I found the rose was overwhelming and distracting. Instead, the rose would be later used in other areas of the design.

(03)

Color

Crafting a cohesive color scheme

In order to craft my color palette I thought deeply about the setting and emotions I want my visitors to feel through my design. I knew my farmers market was going to be held at night so I wanted to showcase colors that would also convey small details such as night skies and the moonlight. However, I also wanted to add elements that evoked the feeling of love. The first thing that came to mind was the color pink.

Conflict of Color Selection:

I was a bit worried that this was not going to work as I had to try a lot of different pink variations to satisfy the WCAG color contrast. I eventually found the right colors but had to be very careful when using it later in my design.

  • Blue colors: reflects the night sky.
  • Pink colors: associated with the rose flowers and the feeling of love.
  • Off-white: reflects the moonlight.
(04)

Poster

Visual storytelling in poster design

To make my poster much more eye-catching and interesting I tried to play with different types of organization to create hierarchy in my design. However, after receiving feedback, I recognized that my initial approach made it hard to read key information, such as the event name, lineup date, and lineup details. This is when I learned that fun design isn't always great. It might look interesting but sometimes it might not be effective. In order to fix this issue I decided to create a much more simple design that follows a column layout. This helped fix the issues with readability and it created more harmony.

After deciding my structure I started to play around with color and fun elements, such as my rose that did not work out in my word mark. For the poster I decided to use the rose to reflect the moon in a dark blue sky with small circle stars. All these elements were carefully chosen to reflect the night setting and romantic atmosphere of my festival.

(05)

Ticket

Festival Ticket Design

I wanted to create a ticket that would fit into someone's wallet. In order to accomplish this I first tried to design a ticket using a business card dimensions (3.5 in X 2 in) but after going through critique rounds I realized that not all business cards fit into every wallet. Additionally I also felt that my design was overly crowded. This causes readability issues for both festival staff and attendees. After taking all these considerations into mind, I redesigned my ticket. First I changed the frame size I was working on by using a universal credit card dimension (2.125 in X 3.37 in). Additionally I also worked on designing both the front and back card utilizing the space more thoughtfully. My intent was to use the front side of the card specifically as a memorable keepsake for attendees while the back side specifically for festival staff to have an easy time to check attendees into the event.

Prototype

Mid Fidelity

While creating my mobile design I aimed to create clear separations between pieces of information to avoid a cluttered appearance. To establish a visual hierarchy, I utilized varying letter spacings and font styles to highlight key elements of the application. Additionally, I incorporated rounded corners in the card design for consistency throughout the user interface. Finally, I included icons across the app to enhance visual appeal and improve user engagement. I presented my first design iteration for critique and got one main issue on the payment and student icon. Many said that the icons were inconsistent between thickness and overall width. However, they enjoyed having the icons as they felt they gave a more friendly look. In order to fix these issues I started to search for different icons that could fit in. In particular, I payed close attention to icon line width and overall width so it can be more consistent.

(09)

Test

Usability Testing

During user testing, participants indicated that the payment screen was somewhat confusing, particularly regarding the student discount section. Feedback highlighted that the wording was unclear and lacked a description of the user actions required. In response, I revised the text and added a brief explanatory note under the discount to clarify the information users would need to provide. Additionally, I received critiques regarding the icons; some appeared inconsistent in thickness. To address this, I adjusted several icons to enhance uniformity across the interface.

(11)

In Reflection

This project was challenging as it was my first time trying to design all elements of an event. The hardest part of the design was creating the poster. I felt that I wanted to create a striking poster design but found out that this was not the correct thinking as it only created lots of readability issues. Although I had a lot of ups and downs creating this work, I realized how small details can make a huge difference into how design is viewed or used. Had I had more time to work on this project I would have liked to explore incorporating dark. This would be a good challenge as the dark theme would force me to think deeply about maintaining the brand layout and also create new harmonies with the existing visual design.

Project Overview

About the LOVE night market festival

“LOVE Night Market Festival” is an imaginary three-day festival held at the University of Washington for food lovers. Through this 10-week project, I worked on creating a visual system that reflects my festival values.

Wordmark

Visual identity through typography

In order to get to my final word mark I considered the following:

  • A feeling of comfort and warmth.
  • A close relationship with the word “Love”.

After considering different fonts I chose to use a serif font named Tapestry. The serif sharp edges reminded me of roses which are associated strongly with the word “Love”. By using tracking I further emphasized the feeling of warmth and comfort.

(03)

Color

Crafting a cohesive color scheme

I wanted to convey a feeling of midnight, moonlight, and love. To accomplish this I chose a monochromatic color palette with primary, secondary, and tertiary colors like off whites and a range of blues and pinks.

  • Blue colors: reflects the night sky.
  • Pink colors: associated with the rose flowers and the feeling of love.
  • Off-white: reflects the moonlight.
(04)

Poster

Visual storytelling in poster design

I utilized a hierarchy to organize the elements in my design. However, after receiving feedback, I recognized that my initial approach hindered the readability of key information, such as the event name, lineup date, and lineup details. To address these issues in my final poster, I employed varying font styles to enhance the emphasis on the critical information that was previously difficult to read.

(05)

Ticket

Festival Ticket Design

I wanted something that could fit in someone's wallet. I tried designing a ticket that uses the business card dimensions (3.5 in X 2 in) but after doing a critique I realized that not all business cards fit into every wallet. This is why I changed it to a universal credit card dimension (2.125 in X 3.37 in). Additionally, I got some feedback on the font size; it was too small and caused trouble with readability. This could be due to the limited space I was working with which is why I made a back and front side. The front side is a memorable keepsake for guests while the back side gives easy readability for staff when people are checking in to the event.

Prototype

Mid Fidelity

I aimed to create clear separations between pieces of information to avoid a cluttered appearance. To establish a visual hierarchy, I utilized varying letter spacing and font styles to highlight key elements of the app. Additionally, I incorporated rounded corners in the card design for consistency throughout the user interface. Finally, I included icons across the app to enhance visual appeal and improve user engagement.

(09)

Test

Usability Testing

During user testing, participants indicated that the payment screen was somewhat confusing, particularly regarding the student discount section. Feedback highlighted that the wording was unclear and lacked a description of the user actions required. In response, I revised the text and added a brief explanatory note under the discount to clarify the information users would need to provide. Additionally, I received critiques regarding the icons; some appeared inconsistent in thickness. To address this, I adjusted several icons to enhance uniformity across the interface.

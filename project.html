Project Overview

About FridgeMinder

FridgeMinder is a trio of devices designed to transform how users manage food in their kitchens, with an emphasis on avoiding food waste. Our trio includes a magnetized fridge-front touch-screen, a camera for the inside of the refrigerator, and a mobile application. We developed FridgeMinder, a high-tech, user-friendly solution to be compatible with any refrigerator.

Define

Problem

“How can we help people eliminate food waste?” We wanted to create a simple solution that can seamlessly integrate into anyone's kitchen while also incorporating smart devices.

(03)

Research

Competitive Analysis

We conducted a competitive analysis that included the Oco security camera, the Smarter FridgeCam, and the Samsung Smart Fridge.

  • The functionality of the Oco security camera was limited; it tracked the contents of a refrigerator without registering the items.
  • Smarter FridgeCam did register items, but was only available for Amazon Fresh users.
  • The Samsung Smart Fridge had an integrated touch screen and registered the contents of a refrigerator, but was very expensive and difficult to move from rental to rental.
(04)

Ideate

Solution

We provide a comprehensive solution to help users prevent food waste. Our product empowers users to avoid food waste by helping them not overbuy while shopping, using our app’s on-the-go fridge camera access or inventory of their items with expiration dates. The fridge-front touch-screen allows users at home to review the contents and expiry dates of their items without having to scour their refrigerator and examine each item. Best of all, this solution is 100% portable, allowing users to pay once and enjoy it in every dorm, apartment, and home they move into.

Conflict ⚠️:

At the start of the project we mainly wanted our targeted users to be college students and did conduct initial semi structured interviews with them revealing that the biggest issue was food waste due to them forgetting about food they had or simply because they were too busy to cook or use up their ingredients. After receiving feedback from industry professionals we learned that the device was not the best for college students as they might not waste money on an expensive device. This is why we pivoted the idea to be more universal not only for college students but for every stage of life and no matter the fridge size.

Ideate

Information Architecture

We worked as a team to organize and structure our user interface to make it intuitive and easy to use. We decided to keep navigation for our landing page simple to avoid overwhelming our user. We also allow users to drill down to find more specific actions or information.

(07)

Prototype

Mid Fidelity

Our mid-fidelity prototype used simple strokes in designing buttons and a clean font style. We introduced some colors to improve the accessibility for our main functionality. This desire to keep our design simple allowed us to collect valuable feedback during user testing.

Design Decision and teamwork conflict ⚠️:

Although it is not very common for mid fidelity prototypes to contain colors, our team wanted to improve users' understanding of the prototype for the user testing step. Our team had a disagreement in this step as we were divided on having color. Some of the team argued against color as it would detract from us from diving deeply into user pain points by wasting unnecessary time in choosing an appropriate color. The other side of the team wanted to add color in order to create a more visually appealing app that would let users differentiate different elements. In the end after discussion with external sources (professors and peers) both sides agreed not to include color at this stage as we need to find core issues plaguing our application design.

(08)

Test

Usability Testing

User testing revealed a few weak points. Users could not immediately navigate back to their previous page, there was a learning curve for deleting and adding items, and the buttons were not obvious.

(09)

Launch

High Fidelity

In order to resolve these issues and confusion that our users had experienced with our low-fidelity prototype we had to meet all the design requirements and how it was solved below:

  • Allow users the freedom to leave the page they are on.
    • FIX: Added back buttons on every page that is not the homepage
  • Reduce the learning curve for deleting.
    • FIX: Skeuomorphism design was used by adding red trash bins next to each inventory item to make fleeting items more intuitive
  • Make the “add item” buttons more obvious
    • FIX: We added a green color to the buttons in order to make the button stand out
(10)

In Reflection

When we began this project, our aim was to tackle food waste. Initially, we were eager to pack our product with as many features as possible. However, through research, user feedback, and continuous refinement, we learned that a simpler approach, focused on what users truly needed, was more effective.

If we had more time, we would deepen our exploration into user experience. Specifically, we aim to gain a better understanding of the interaction between users and the FridgeMinder camera and digital display. This insight would be crucial for identifying necessary adjustments and enhancements.
